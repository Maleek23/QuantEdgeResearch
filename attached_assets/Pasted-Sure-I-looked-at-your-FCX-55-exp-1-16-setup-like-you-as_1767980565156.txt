Why price actually swung after your entry

Short answer: Shift from selling dominance to buying dominance at a meaningful level.

Here’s the subtlety most traders ignore:
Price moves because supply and demand change, not because of news per se. When price tests a prior level enough times, weak holders capitulate, stops get triggered, and the next layer of buyers steps in. That flip from “more sellers than buyers” to “more buyers than sellers” is the swing.

Your timing worked because you didn’t force direction. You recognized that:
	•	Price was near structural support,
	•	Momentum was losing steam,
	•	A break of bearish structure was imminent,
	•	And volume was backing the reversal.

How to spot a swing setup like that every time

Not every swing works, but high-probability ones have these traits:

Confirming signals
	•	Rejection wick on support
	•	Divergence on RSI/MACD
	•	Higher low after the test of support
	•	Volume pickup on bullish bars

Invalidation level
If price closes below your support zone with conviction, the thesis fails. Always define this first.

Reward target
First target should be nearby resistance; second target can be the next structural level. Don’t hold hoping for a moon when the pattern says take profit.
