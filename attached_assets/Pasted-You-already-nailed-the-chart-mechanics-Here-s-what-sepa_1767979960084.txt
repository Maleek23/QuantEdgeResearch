## **1. Implied Volatility is the Real Undercurrent**

Your chart says "bounce." The options chain says *"we're pricing a ±18% move by expiry."*

**Check this BEFORE you buy:**
- **IV Rank/IV Percentile**: If it's above 70th percentile, you're buying expensive premium. A bounce to $24 might still lose money if IV collapses.
- **IV vs HV**: If implied is trading 2x historical, the market's already scared. You need a *parabolic* move, not a disciplined bounce.
- **Earnings vol crush**: That 1/26 expiry is begging for it. Post-earnings IV can drop 40-60% overnight. Your delta gains evaporate if vega kicks you in the teeth.

**Action**: If IV > 50%, consider a vertical spread to short some premium. Don't be the sucker holding naked calls into vol collapse.

## **2. Time Decay is Non-Negotiable Math**

You mentioned theta-burning. Let's quantify it.

- **Current theta** on a 21-strike call: likely -$0.04/day now, accelerating to -$0.12/day in the final week.
- **Breakeven acceleration**: Price needs to rise ~$0.50/week just to *offset* decay.

Your "hold above 19.5" invalidation is fine, but **time invalidation** is stricter: if you're not above $22 by 1/19, you're statistically dead.

**Action**: Set a time stop, not just a price stop. If it hasn't moved by Jan 15th, exit. Don't hope.

## **3. Position Sizing: The Only Thing That Actually Prevents Blowups**

Your analysis is solid. Still doesn't matter if you're 30% allocated.

**Rule for mean reversion trades**: 
- Risk 0.5–1% of *total account* per trade. 
- Your stop is $19 (delta ≈ -0.30 on a 21C). 
- If call premium is $1.50, you need position size = (0.5% × Account) / ($1.50 - $0.60 expected residual). 

**This keeps you in the game when 3 of these fail sequentially** — which they will.
